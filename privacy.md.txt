## 1. Introduction

**1.1** Data Fluent Global (Pty) Ltd ("**we**," "**us**," "**our**") respects your privacy and is committed to protecting the personal information you share with us.

**1.2** This Privacy Policy explains what personal information we collect, how we use it, with whom we share it, how long we retain it, and the rights you have in respect of your personal information.

**1.3** This Privacy Policy applies to personal information collected through our website at [https://datafluent.global](https://datafluent.global) and any subdomains, products, and services we operate (collectively, the "**Services**").

**1.4** We process personal information in accordance with the **Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013 of the Republic of South Africa ("POPIA")**, and where applicable, the **EU General Data Protection Regulation 2016/679 ("GDPR")** and the **UK General Data Protection Regulation ("UK GDPR")**.

## 2. Information We Collect

We collect personal information in the following ways:

### 2.1 Information you provide directly

  (a) **Account and purchase information:** name, email address, organisation name, role/title, billing address, country, and any information you provide when registering, purchasing, or contacting us.

  (b) **Payment information:** payment-card details are collected and processed directly by our third-party payment processor (see clause 6). We do not store full payment-card numbers on our systems. We retain limited transaction metadata (such as transaction ID, amount, date, and last four digits of the card) for accounting and audit purposes.

  (c) **Communications:** the content of emails, support requests, enquiry-form submissions, and other communications you send to us.

  (d) **Marketing preferences:** your subscription status for newsletters, lead magnets, product updates, and other marketing communications.

### 2.2 Information collected automatically

  (a) **Usage data:** pages visited, time on page, referrer URL, navigation paths, and similar information collected through analytics tools.

  (b) **Device and technical data:** IP address, browser type and version, operating system, device type, screen resolution, language preferences, and timestamps of access.

  (c) **Cookies and similar technologies:** see clause 10.

### 2.3 Information from third parties

  (a) **Payment confirmation:** transaction status, success/failure indicators, and limited customer details received from our payment processor to confirm purchases.

  (b) **Public sources:** professional information that you make publicly available (for example, on a public LinkedIn profile) where relevant to a commercial enquiry you have initiated with us.

## 3. How We Use Your Information

We use personal information for the following purposes:

  (a) **Service delivery:** to deliver the products and services you have purchased, including granting access to licensed materials, sending order confirmations, and providing support.

  (b) **Customer communication:** to respond to enquiries, support requests, and other communications.

  (c) **Marketing (with consent):** to send newsletters, product updates, and offers where you have opted in to receive them. You may withdraw consent at any time.

  (d) **Service improvement:** to analyse usage patterns, troubleshoot technical issues, and improve our Website, products, and services.

  (e) **Legal and regulatory compliance:** to comply with applicable laws, regulations, court orders, or governmental requests, and to detect, prevent, or address fraud, security, or technical issues.

  (f) **Contract performance:** to administer customer accounts, process payments, issue invoices, and enforce our agreements with you.

  (g) **Legitimate business interests:** to operate, protect, and grow our business in ways that are not overridden by your privacy rights.

## 4. Legal Basis for Processing

### 4.1 Under POPIA

We process personal information on one or more of the following bases set out in section 11 of POPIA:

  (a) you have given consent;
  (b) the processing is necessary to conclude or perform a contract with you;
  (c) the processing complies with a legal obligation imposed on us;
  (d) the processing protects a legitimate interest of yours;
  (e) the processing is necessary for our legitimate interests or those of a third party, where these are not overridden by your privacy rights.

### 4.2 Under GDPR / UK GDPR (where applicable)

For data subjects in the European Union or United Kingdom, we rely on the following lawful bases under Article 6 of the GDPR / UK GDPR:

  (a) **Consent** (Article 6(1)(a)) — for marketing communications, optional analytics, and cookies that are not strictly necessary.
  (b) **Contractual necessity** (Article 6(1)(b)) — to deliver products and services you have purchased.
  (c) **Legal obligation** (Article 6(1)(c)) — for tax, accounting, and regulatory record-keeping.
  (d) **Legitimate interests** (Article 6(1)(f)) — for service improvement, fraud prevention, and direct marketing to existing customers regarding similar products, balanced against your rights.

## 5. Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell your personal information. We disclose personal information only in the following circumstances:

  (a) **Service providers:** to third-party operators who process personal information on our behalf to deliver our Services. These include payment processors, email and marketing platforms, hosting and cloud-infrastructure providers, analytics providers, and customer-support tools. Such providers are bound by contractual obligations to process personal information only as instructed and to maintain appropriate security measures.

  (b) **Legal requirements:** where required by law, court order, or governmental request, or where necessary to protect our legal rights, prevent fraud, or address security issues.

  (c) **Business transfers:** in connection with a merger, acquisition, corporate reorganisation, or sale of all or substantially all of our assets, in which case personal information may be transferred to the successor entity subject to this Privacy Policy or an equivalent successor policy.

  (d) **With your consent:** in any other circumstance where you have specifically consented to the disclosure.

## 7. International Transfers

**7.1** Some of our service providers are located outside the Republic of South Africa, including in the European Union, the United States, and other jurisdictions. When we transfer personal information outside South Africa, we ensure such transfers comply with section 72 of POPIA, including by:

  (a) transferring only to recipients in jurisdictions with equivalent data protection laws;
  (b) entering into binding contractual arrangements requiring the recipient to comply with comparable protections; or
  (c) obtaining your consent to the transfer where appropriate.

**7.2** For data subjects in the European Union or United Kingdom, we rely on Standard Contractual Clauses or other approved transfer mechanisms under Articles 44–49 of the GDPR / UK GDPR for transfers outside the EEA or UK.

## 8. Data Retention

**8.1** We retain personal information only for as long as is necessary for the purposes for which it was collected, including:

  (a) **Active customer records:** for the duration of the customer relationship and for a reasonable period thereafter.

  (b) **Transactional and accounting records:** for the period required by South African tax and accounting law (currently five years from the end of the relevant tax period under the Tax Administration Act 28 of 2011).

  (c) **Marketing contacts:** until you withdraw consent or for a reasonable period of inactivity, after which records are deleted or anonymised.

  (d) **Support communications:** for a reasonable period to enable continued support and quality review.

**8.2** Where retention is no longer justified, we delete, destroy, or de-identify personal information in accordance with our internal retention schedule.

## 9. Your Rights

### 9.1 Rights under POPIA

As a data subject under POPIA, you have the right to:

  (a) **Be informed** that we hold personal information about you and what information we hold;
  (b) **Access** the personal information we hold about you;
  (c) **Correct** personal information that is inaccurate, irrelevant, excessive, out of date, incomplete, misleading, or unlawfully obtained;
  (d) **Delete** personal information that is no longer necessary or that we are required to delete;
  (e) **Object** to the processing of your personal information in certain circumstances, including objecting to direct marketing;
  (f) **Withdraw consent** at any time, where processing is based on consent;
  (g) **Complain** to the Information Regulator (South Africa) — see clause 14.

### 9.2 Additional rights under GDPR / UK GDPR (where applicable)

For data subjects in the European Union or United Kingdom, additional rights include:

  (a) **Data portability** — to receive your personal information in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format;
  (b) **Restriction of processing** — to require us to limit how we use your personal information in certain circumstances;
  (c) **Not to be subject to automated decision-making** — including profiling, that produces legal or similarly significant effects, except as permitted by law.

### 9.3 Exercising your rights

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at **info@datafluent.global**. We will respond within the timeframes required by applicable law (in most cases, within thirty (30) days under POPIA, or one (1) month under GDPR / UK GDPR).

We may require verification of your identity before processing a request. Requests that are manifestly unfounded, excessive, or repetitive may attract a reasonable fee or may be refused, as permitted by law.

## 10. Cookies and Similar Technologies

**10.1** We use cookies and similar technologies (such as web beacons, pixels, and local storage) on the Website to:

  (a) maintain session state and remember user preferences;
  (b) measure Website performance and usage;
  (c) deliver and measure marketing campaigns, where applicable;
  (d) ensure security and detect abuse.

**10.2 Categories of cookies we use:**

  (a) **Strictly necessary cookies** — required for the Website to function (no consent required).
  (b) **Performance and analytics cookies** — to understand Website usage (consent required where applicable).
  (c) **Marketing cookies** — for advertising and retargeting (consent required where applicable).

**10.3** Where consent is required for non-essential cookies, we present a cookie banner allowing you to accept, reject, or customise your preferences. You can also manage cookies through your browser settings.

**10.4** Disabling cookies may affect the functionality of the Website.

**11.1** We implement reasonable technical and organisational security measures designed to protect personal information against unauthorised access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. Measures include:
